Kenya’s journey toward universal sanitation access is being held back by a systemic crisis of fragmentation, under-coordination, and missed opportunities. Each unrecorded, unshared, and unscaled effort adds to the growing gap—transforming what should be a coordinated national movement into an unfinished agenda.

From Systemic Challenge to Scalable Solution

The sanitation sector’s vital work often remains confined to unpublicized silos. This isolation prevents Kenya from moving beyond incremental progress toward scalable, equitable access models.

The SaniBook is built to change that—by centralizing data, interventions, and impact stories from across the sector, enabling collaboration and shared accountability toward achieving SDG 6.2.
