The Gaps in Technology Adoption in Sanitation

In an era defined by disruptive innovation, Kenya’s sanitation sector stands at a crossroads. The persistent gaps in technology adoption are not mere obstacles—they represent a profound, untapped opportunity to redefine how we deliver sanitation solutions that are inclusive, scalable, and sustainable.

Bridging the Divide

The time for incremental change is over. We are at a critical inflection point where innovative, decentralized technologies meet a fragmented regulatory landscape. Addressing this requires a bold, systems-based paradigm shift—one that overcomes approval bottlenecks, outdated mindsets, and institutional inertia.

By championing single-window clearance systems, encouraging a mindset shift toward non-sewered solutions, and leveraging self-regulation to codify success, we can transform technology from an isolated achievement into a catalyst for sector-wide progress.
